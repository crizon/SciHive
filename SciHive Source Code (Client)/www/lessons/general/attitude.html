Scientist Attitudes

As an aspiring young scientist, you should possess the scientific attitudes toward solving problems so that you will become successful in finding the answer to a problem. Likewise, you should use a systematic method in answering problems and follow some precautionary measures in doing experiments.
Here are some of the good attitudes an individual must posses:





h
Careful Judgement

Scientists do not easily jump to a conclusion. Their judgement is based on facts and reliable information.

Creativity

Scientist are sensitive to problems and can generate original ideas. They are able to find new and alternative answers to problems.

Critical mindedness

Scientist are willing to evaluate evidence presented to support a conclusion. They prefer accuracy of facts and reliability of source of information.

Curiosity

Scientists never stop learning. They want to know the why and how of things.

Humility

Scientists are humble and do not boast their achievements. They do not belittle others whom they think do not meet their standards.

Intellectual Honesty

Scientists give reports truthfully and acknowledge sources of information. They do not claim the works of others, and they give credit where it is due.

Objectivity

Scientists record actual observations and data accurately. They interpret data based on actual observations and experiments. They verify the consistency of observations.

Patience

Scientists have the ability to spend more times or days of work to finish a task and get the accurate result.

Resourcefulness

Scientists think of possible alternative solutions to problems they are working on. They find alternative materials to be used for their experiment.

Rationality

Scientist believe that in this world, things happen for a reason. They only facts and statements that are supported by proof.
