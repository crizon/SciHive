The three main subdivisions of science are Physical Sciences, Earth Sciences, and Life Sciences.





h

Physical Sciences

The study about nonliving matter. Some of the sciences that fall under this category are:

Chemistry
Deals with the study of the composition, structure, form, and physical and chemical properties of matter. Chemistry has several other subcategories like organic chemistry, inorganic chemistry, nuclear chemistry, and biochemistry.

Physics
Deals with the study of matter and energy, including light, sound, electricity, magnetism, radiation, and motion.





h

Earth Sciences

Deals with the scientific study of Earth. The branches of science that comprise Earth Science are the following:

Geology
Branch of science that involves the study of the origin, history, evolution, and structure of the earth's crust. It also involves the examination of soil and rocks.

Paleontology
The scientific study of prehistoric life, based mainly on fossils of animals and plants.

Meteorology
The study of atmosphere that focuses on weather processes and forecasting.

Astronomy
The study of celestial bodies such as stars, comets, planets and galaxies, and phenomena that originate outside Earth's atmospere such as cosmic background radiation, gamma ray bursts and supernova explosions.





h

Life Sciences

Deals with the study of living organisms. The science under this category focuses on the study of internal body functions and structures of body cells and tissues.

Biology
The study of living organisms and their vital processes. It encompasses all the various aspects of life like the structure and function of the different organs.

Botany
The study of plants, including the properties and life phenomena exhibited by a plant, plant type, or plant group.

Zoology
The study of all aspects of animal life.

Ecology
The study about the relationship of living organisms with each other and their interaction with their surroundings.

Genetics
The study of the genetic makeup of living organisms.
