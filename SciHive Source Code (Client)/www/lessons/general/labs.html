Laboratory Apparatus and Instruments

Experiments are commonly conducted in a science class. Pupils design an experiment to investigate an area of science by means of principles and methods scientists use every day.

Whenever you perform an experiment, it is important for you to know the right instruments and apparatuses you need. It is also important for you to know how to use them properly.





Bunsen Burner

A small adjustable gas burner used in laboratories. Named after Robert Bunsen.




Test Tube

A thin glass tube closed at one end, used to hold small amounts of material for laboratory testing or experiments.




Test Tube Rack

A rack use to carry certain numbers of test tube.




Funnel

A tube or pipe that is wide at the top and narrow at the bottom, used for guiding liquid or powder into a small opening.




Erlenmeyer Flask

A conical, flat-bottomed laboratory flask with a narrow neck. late 19th cent.: named after Emil Erlenmeyer (1825â€“1909), German chemist.




Thermometer

An instrument for measuring and indicating temperature, typically one consisting of a narrow, hermetically sealed glass tube marked with graduations and having at one end a bulb containing mercury or alcohol that expands and contracts in the tube with heating and cooling.




Alcohol Lamp

A lamp use burn material in a certain temperature.




Wire Gauze

A wire gauze is a sheet of thin metal that has net-like patterns or a wire mesh. Wire gauze is placed on the support ring that is attached to the retort stand between the Bunsen burner and the glassware to support the beakers, flasks, or other glassware during heating.




Graduated Cylinder

A tall narrow container with volume scale used especially for measuring liquids.




Beaker

A lipped cylindrical glass container for laboratory use.




Florence Flask

A round usually flat-bottomed laboratory vessel with a long neck.




Petri Dish

A shallow, circular, transparent dish with a flat lid, used for the culture of microorganisms.




Dropper

A short glass tube with a rubber bulb at one end and a tiny hole at the other, for measuring out drops of medicine or other liquids.




Microscope

An optical instrument used for viewing very small objects, such as mineral samples or animal or plant cells, typically magnified several hundred times.




Tongs

An instrument with two movable arms that are joined at one end, used for picking up and holding things.




Cover Slip

A small, thin piece of glass used to cover and protect a specimen on a microscope slide.




Magnifying Glass

A lens that produces an enlarged image, typically set in a frame with a handle and used to examine small or finely detailed things such as fingerprints, stamps, and fine print.




Ring Stand

A metal stand consisting of a long upright rod attached to a heavy rectangular base that is used with rings and clamps for supporting laboratory apparatus.




Stirring Rod

A glass stirring rod, glass rod, stirring rod or stir rod is a piece of laboratory equipment used to mix chemicals and liquids for laboratory purposes. They are usually made of solid glass, about the thickness and slightly longer than a drinking straw, with rounded ends.




Rubber Stopper

Or a rubber bung is mainly used in chemical laboratory in combination with flasks and test tube and also for fermentation in winery.




Glass Slide

A a thin, flat, rectangular piece of glass that is used as a platform for microscopic specimen observation. A typical glass slide usually measures 25 mm wide by 75 mm, or 1 inch by 3 inches long, and is designed to fit under the stage clips on a microscope stage.
